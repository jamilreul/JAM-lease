How Does Leasing Work?

Leasing is a financial agreement where one party, the lessor, provides an asset to another party, the lessee, for use over a specified period of time, the lease term, in exchange for periodic payments. The lease term is typically shorter than the assetâ€™s economic life. The lessor retains ownership of the asset but transfers the right to use the asset to the lessee. This allows the lessee to use the asset without the need for large initial capital outlays.
